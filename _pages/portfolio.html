First Statistics, then Computational Science and now Computational Biology ... I am a statistician and well educated in scientific computing. I rather call myself a computational Statistician which I believe better represents my skills and education. I have a Master's degree from Informatics Institute, ITU , perhaps the place to get an Informatics education in Turkey. Since then, I had two excellent mentors that shaped my academic career as a scientist, Elvan Ceyhan and Adem Tekin. I am fortunate to have been introduced to the magnificent world of Graph Theory and its applications to Computational Statistics by Prof.Ceyhan.

I am fascinated by Networks and graphs, I enjoy their complexity and interpretability, but now I have decided to go on a path to discovering what computational biology has to offer! I see Biological Networks as another milestone in my journey!
